Best of Extreme Metal 2025

Accurate genre and style assignments seem impossible for many of these bands; throw the dice and don't trust my vibe attributions.

Death Metal / Blackened Death

Band Album Style & Notes
Ancient Death Ego Dissolution Progressive / technical death metal, cosmic theme and feel.
Maybe the most classic technical death metal release on this list; yet feels unique in how skillfully the proggy moments are woven together.
Filth Time To Rot Old-school death metal.
Primitive and unforgiving with a few uplifting moments. One of the albums I returned surprisingly often to in recent months.
Floating Hesitating Lights Progressive death metal, some of the darker post-punk stuff mixed in.
First I was a bit skeptical, but I appreciate the style now. Take "The Waking": strong on the riff and proggy elements - then it switches as if in depressing The Cure songs and partly back again. Neat!
Patristic Catechesis Blackened death metal.
At times so fast and energetic that it feels like it might overthrow itself.
QRIXKUOR The Womb Of The World Experimental / symphonic death metal.
The disordered madness together with the epic, echoing sound evokes a pretty hellish atmoshphere. Well done!
Sallow Moth Mossbane Lantern Progressive / technical / brutal death metal, with some sci-fi oddness.
A release I enjoyed very much; technical and surprisingly playful genre variations, yet it doesn't lose any of its aggressive force.
Teitanblood From The Visceral Abyss Chaotic black/death
At times grunts from deepest hell, at other times super aggressive, fast assault. Great at all times.
Veilburner Longing For Triumph, Reeking Of Tragedy Progressive blackened death metal.
A very weird one indeed. Starting with its cover... Labyrinthine stuff. At best (take "That Which Crypts Howls Grandeur"), I was reminded of the some of the greatest aggressive experimental works of recent years, such as Morkera, and sometimes of the crazier parts of Dødheimsgard. In any case, it grew on me; probably one of the best releases of last year.

Black Metal - Classic

Band Album Style
Abduction Existentialismus UK black metal.
Perhaps the straightest black metal record on the list; more expressive in tone though. Sometimes almost too epic in style for me; The cover reminds me eerily of Meshuggah's Obzen.
Volahn Popol Vuh California black metal.
Very fast, still somewhat melodic, some mystical parts. I listened especially to the excellent outro track a lot of times by now. Reminds of everything great about more classic double bass drum blasting.

Black Metal - Chaos / Dissonant / Avant-garde

Band Album Style
Smiqra Rɡyaɡ̇dźé! Avant-garde black metal.
Mathcore chaos from China. Probably not really black metal, has more of free jazzy freedom to it.
Blut Aus Nord Ethereal Horizons Progressive / experimental black metal.
Has more of a cosmic, distant feeling to it than some of the more psychedelic Blut aus Nord releases the years before. And definitly more symphonic. Probably not my favorite of theirs, but as with all their releases, it's still better than just about anything else and definitely worth checking out.
Imperial Triumphant Goldstar Avant-garde / experimental / jazz metal.
The well-known Art Deco virtuosos from New York with their version of Metropolis, I guess. Freewheeling, highly unpredictable stuff. As usual, overwhelming; don't be put off by the self-indulgent style. The reward is a quite challenging listening experience.
Death Obvious Death Obvious Avant-garde death/black/doom metal.
Something new from.. Finland. And it is so often with these Norderners, it's pretty batshit insane. Demented and disorienting.
Esoctrilihum Ghostigmatah - Spiritual Rites Of The Psychopomp Abxulöm Mystical avant-garde black metal
Yey, another one of my favorites of last year. Complex black/death amalgam. At times, it reaches very weird symphonic heights - yet, it doesn't fail to suck you back into some nightmarish netherworld.
Grey Aura Zwart Vierkant: Slotstuk Avant-garde black metal
Sadly missed out them when I was at Roadburn this year. Chaotic & abrasive, and they also have the same vibe as Dødheimsgard's crazier phases - nothing but the highest compliment, of course.

Black Metal - Atmospheric & Other

Band Album Style
Yellow Eyes Confusion Gate Atmospheric black metal with ambient/folk/industrial textures.
An excellent release of an excellent band - one that I was lucky to see three times this year at Roadburn. Very gripping, masterful blending of genres. I even like the Wicker-Man-in-the-distance folk aura I get from the cover.
Starer Ancient Monuments And Modern Sadness Symphonic / atmospheric modern black metal.
Lazer Throne Tomb Of The Lunar Oracle Cosmic atmospheric black metal.
Tombs Feral Darkness Black metal with post-metal/sludge/hardcore edges.
Fauna Ochre & Ash Cascadian / ritualistic atmospheric black metal, with slow funeral-doom passages; not “classic” enough for subcategory 1.
Terzij de Horde Our Breath Is Not Ours Alone Urgent hardcore-inflected black metal with melodic intensity and post-hardcore/screamo fire.

Doom / Sludge / Crust

Band Album Style
Weeping Sores The Convalescence Agonies Death/doom metal.
Morast Fentanyl Blackened doom/death metal.
Haggus Destination Extinction Mincecore / grindcore / hardcore / goregrind; crust-punk-adjacent more than death metal proper.
Tesa Interval Instrumental post-metal / experimental metal, also close to progressive sludge/post-hardcore/post-metal.

The Unidentifiable Rest

Band Album Style
Pyrex Body Raw power punk / hardcore punk.
CKRAFT Uncommon Grounds Jazz-fusion / progressive metal / avant-prog metal; more “rest” than black/death/doom.
Neptunian Maximalism Le Sacre Du Soleil Invaincu Avant-garde drone / free jazz / ritual ambient / Hindustani-classical heaviness; doom-adjacent in weight, but better under “rest.”
